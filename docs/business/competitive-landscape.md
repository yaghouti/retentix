# Retentix – Competitive Landscape

This document positions Retentix against the most common alternatives used by EU-based companies to address GDPR erasure and retention obligations.

The comparison focuses on **economic reality, operational burden, and audit defensibility**, not feature checklists.

---

## Market Categories

Organizations typically fall into one of three approaches:

1. Compliance Platforms (e.g. OneTrust)
2. Data Discovery & Governance Platforms (e.g. BigID)
3. Internal Scripts & Ad-hoc Engineering

Retentix occupies a **fourth category**: *policy execution infrastructure*.

---

## 1. OneTrust (Compliance Platforms)

### What it is good at
- Policy documentation
- Risk assessments
- DPIAs and questionnaires
- Compliance workflows and reporting

### Structural limitations
- Primarily **process and documentation oriented**
- Limited technical execution capabilities
- Relies on integrations or manual steps for deletion

### Economic reality
- High license cost
- Requires significant internal process work
- Does not remove engineering execution effort

### Positioning vs Retentix

| Aspect | OneTrust | Retentix |
|------|---------|----------|
| Primary role | Compliance management | Policy execution |
| Technical depth | Low–Medium | High |
| Deletes data | Indirect / manual | Deterministic |
| Audit evidence | Process-based | Execution-based |

**Summary:** OneTrust documents intent. Retentix executes it.

---

## 2. BigID (Data Discovery & Governance)

### What it is good at
- Data discovery and classification
- Mapping personal data locations
- Privacy risk insights

### Structural limitations
- Focuses on *finding* data, not enforcing actions
- Execution often delegated to downstream tools or scripts
- Complex to deploy and operate

### Economic reality
- Enterprise-grade pricing
- Long onboarding cycles
- Ongoing operational overhead

### Positioning vs Retentix

| Aspect | BigID | Retentix |
|------|-------|----------|
| Primary role | Data discovery | Policy execution |
| Runtime execution | No | Yes |
| Operational footprint | Heavy | Lightweight |
| Proof of erasure | Partial | Full |

**Summary:** BigID answers *where data exists*. Retentix answers *what was enforced*.

---

## 3. Custom Scripts & Internal Tooling

### Why teams choose this
- Full control
- No vendor dependency
- Appears cheaper initially

### Structural limitations
- Knowledge silos
- High maintenance cost
- Fragile to schema and system changes
- No standardized audit output

### Economic reality
- Hidden, recurring engineering cost
- Increasing compliance risk over time
- Difficult to justify during audits

### Positioning vs Retentix

| Aspect | Custom Scripts | Retentix |
|------|---------------|----------|
| Initial cost | Low | Moderate |
| Long-term cost | High | Predictable |
| Audit defensibility | Weak | Strong |
| Reusability | Low | High |

**Summary:** Scripts solve incidents. Retentix solves the class of problems.

---

## Retentix Positioning (Clear and Defensible)

Retentix is **not**:
- A compliance management platform
- A data discovery scanner
- A consulting engagement

Retentix **is**:
- A deterministic execution engine
- Installed inside customer infrastructure
- Designed to produce repeatable, auditable outcomes

---

## One-Sentence Competitive Positioning

> *OneTrust documents compliance, BigID discovers data, scripts attempt deletion — Retentix is the system that proves enforcement.*

## Buyer Guidance

| If your main problem is… | You likely need… |
|-------------------------|----------------|
| Documentation & policies | OneTrust |
| Knowing where data lives | BigID |
| Proving data was erased everywhere | Retentix |

---

## Conclusion

Retentix complements existing compliance and governance platforms rather than replacing them.

Its economic value lies in removing recurring engineering work and transforming regulatory obligations into **provable, infrastructure-level execution**.
