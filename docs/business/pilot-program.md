# Retentix – 30-Day Paid Pilot Program

**Audience:** EU CTOs, Heads of Engineering, DPOs  
**Price:** €5,000 – €10,000 (credited toward annual license)  
**Duration:** 30 calendar days  
**Scope:** One controlled production-like environment

> **Background:** See [Economic Justification](../product/economic-justification.md) for why companies need Retentix and ROI analysis.

---

## Purpose of the Pilot

The Retentix pilot is **not a trial** and **not a demo**.

It is a short, paid engagement designed to answer one question:

> *Can this organization trust Retentix to execute legally irreversible GDPR actions deterministically in production?*

The pilot focuses on **execution correctness, auditability, and operational fit** — not feature breadth.

---

## Commercial Terms

- **Fee:**
  - €5,000 (single use-case)
  - €10,000 (multiple executions)

- **Payment:** upfront, invoiced
- **Credit:** 100% credited toward first annual license if converted within 60 days
- **No auto-renewal**

This structure filters for serious buyers and minimizes vendor support load.

---

## In Scope (Strictly Limited)

### Environment

- 1 environment (prod *or* prod-like)
- 1 database or data store
- Read/write access only for pilot scope

### Executions

Choose **one or two**:

- Time-based data retention
- RTBF (Right to Be Forgotten / erasure)
- Field-level masking

### Operational Model

- CLI-only usage
- Docker-based deployment
- Dry-run first, execution only after sign-off

---

## Out of Scope (Explicitly)

- No UI or dashboards
- No data discovery or scanning
- No custom policy logic
- No long-term support obligations
- No SLA

This keeps the pilot deterministic and low-risk.

---

## Pilot Timeline (30 Days)

### Week 1 – Setup & Validation

- Deliver Docker image
- Provide sample policies
- Validate license
- Run policy validation + dry-run

**Deliverable:** validated policy + dry-run audit log

---

### Week 2 – Controlled Execution

- Execute selected retention / erasure / masking
- Capture full audit logs
- Verify deterministic behavior

**Deliverable:** execution report + audit artifacts

---

### Week 3 – Review & Trust Building

- Engineering review of execution behavior
- DPO review of audit trail
- Optional internal security review

**Deliverable:** internal sign-off or findings

---

### Week 4 – Commercial Decision

- Pilot summary
- License recommendation
- Conversion decision

**Deliverable:** go / no-go decision

---

## Success Criteria (Objective)

The pilot is considered successful if:

- Executions behave deterministically
- All destructive actions are auditable
- Fail-fast validation prevents unsafe execution
- Engineering can operate Retentix without vendor intervention
- DPO can sign off on the audit output

No subjective satisfaction metrics are used.
